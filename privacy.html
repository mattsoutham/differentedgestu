Privacy & Cookie Policy

How we collect, use, and protect your information.

Last updated: 30 June 2026

This policy explains how Different Edge Studio ("we", "us", "our") collects and uses information when you visit differentedgestudio.com or contact us. We are committed to protecting your privacy in line with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

1. Who we are

Different Edge Studio is a B2B video production and sales enablement studio based in the United Kingdom. Our main point of contact for data matters is Dan at dan@differentedgestudio.com.

2. What information we collect

Information you give us

When you fill in a form on our website (such as the Sales Video Assessment or any contact form), we collect:

  • Your name
  • Your email address
  • Your business size (where asked)
  • Your assessment responses (where applicable)

Information collected automatically

When you visit our website, we use analytics tools that collect data about how you use the site. This may include your IP address (anonymised), browser type, pages visited, time spent on pages, and general location. This is collected via cookies — see Section 5 for details.

3. How we use your information

We use the information you provide solely for the purpose you submitted it for. Specifically:

  • If you complete the Sales Video Assessment, we use your email to send you your results and to follow up about how we may be able to help your business.
  • If you contact us directly, we use your details to respond to your enquiry.
  • We do not add you to any marketing lists without your explicit consent.
  • We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with third parties for their own marketing purposes.

4. Legal basis for processing

Under UK GDPR, we process your personal data on the following bases:

  • Legitimate interests — to respond to enquiries and follow up on assessment results you have requested.
  • Consent — for any optional marketing communications.

5. Cookies

Our website uses cookies to help us understand how visitors use the site and to improve your experience. A cookie is a small text file stored on your device.

Analytics cookies

We use Google Analytics to collect anonymised data about traffic and usage patterns. Google Analytics uses cookies to track sessions. The data collected is aggregated and does not identify you personally. You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking via the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

We also use Microsoft Clarity, a behavioural analytics tool that records anonymised session data including mouse movements, clicks, and scrolling. This helps us understand how people interact with our pages. Microsoft Clarity may use cookies and local storage. You can learn more about how Clarity handles data at Microsoft's Privacy Statement.

Essential cookies

Some cookies are strictly necessary for the website to function. These cannot be disabled.

Managing cookies

You can control and delete cookies through your browser settings. Note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of the site. For more information on managing cookies, visit aboutcookies.org.

6. Data retention

We keep enquiry and assessment data for as long as is reasonably necessary to respond to your request and maintain a record of our correspondence, typically no longer than 24 months from last contact. You can request deletion at any time — see Section 8.

7. Third-party services

We use the following third-party services which may process data on our behalf:

  • Google Analytics — website analytics (Google LLC, US-based, governed by EU-US Data Privacy Framework)
  • Microsoft Clarity — behavioural analytics (Microsoft Corporation, US-based)
  • Mailgun — transactional email delivery (Sinch, EU-region servers)
  • Calendly — appointment scheduling (Calendly LLC, US-based)

Each of these services has their own privacy policy and data handling practices. We only use services that provide adequate data protection safeguards.

8. Your rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your data
  • Object to or restrict our processing of your data
  • Withdraw consent at any time (where processing is based on consent)
  • Lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk

To exercise any of these rights, email us at dan@differentedgestudio.com and we will respond within 30 days.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The date at the top of this page shows when it was last revised. Continued use of the site following any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

10. Contact

For any questions about this policy or how we handle your data, contact Dan at dan@differentedgestudio.com.
